  • Backend
    • 3 types of database relationships
      • One to One: One record in one table links to another record in another table. The one is usually called a primary key.
      • One to Many: One record in one table links to multiple records in another table. The many is usually called a foreign key.
      • Many to Many: Multiple records in one table links to multiple records in another table. This requires a junction table.

  • Legal Requirements
    • GDPR/DPA
    • PECR (Cookie Policies)
      • DUAA ammended PECR in 2025
      • changed max fine form £500,000 to inline with GDPR/DPA
      • Exceptions for low risk cookies not requiring otp-in
    • Consumer protection laws
      • Consumer Rights Act 2015:
      • Users have the right to repair or replacement of faulty digital content
      • A 30-day window exists for a full refund on faulty digital goods
      • If your digital product damages a user's device, the trader is liable if they failed to exercise reasonable care and skill
      • You could also briefly mention the Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013, which govern the right to cancel online purchases within 14 days
    • Equality Act 2010
    • IP/Copyright laws
      • Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988 (CDPA)
      • Website code, UI design, written content, and logos are all protected as soon as they're created
      • Using third-party assets (images, fonts, libraries) without a valid licence is infringement — check licences
      • Trademarks protect your product name/logo and require registration via the UK IPO
